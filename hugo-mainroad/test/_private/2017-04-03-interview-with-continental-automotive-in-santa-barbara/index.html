Interview with Continental Automotive in Santa Barbara

April 03, 2017

The company

This company was founded in Santa Barbara and got acquired by Continental Automotive, which is the second largest automotive supplier in the world based in Germany. People know Continental mostly because of its tire product, or maybe his automobile electronics system. Actually, the branch got acquired is only the commercial part of its original company, the other part is for military which stays independent.

The branch itself just start to work on the product, and hasn’t taped out yet, despite of some testcase basically for the pixel processing unit itself. And most of the members are electronics engineer, which is on board level, and physical engineers.

The team

The manager of the team, Fritz, is some kind of people manager type of guy. I don’t think he knows too much about IC design, don’t know much about his background in electronics design. But he seems nice and funny.

The technique lead of the team, Marty, has been in the industry for 30 years, and more leaning to the analog side. He himself draws schemetics and layouts under 40nm. The tools are SPICE level mostly. He knows about the circuit, but might be a little bit old fasion in terms of design methodology. So if I have to use this old fasion methodology more, I’m not sure what’s it going to be good for myself.

The other guys, 2 Germans 1 local, are pretty nice. The older guy named Joe is kind of funny, who has been working for military back in time.

The location

The most concern I’m having is the location, Santa Barbara area. There aren’t many IC design companies in this area, so won’t be good for future career advance. Marty himself just moved from out of state, while his wife is still not here with him. I think the offer must be pretty good for him.

The location of the office is pretty amazing. It’s just along the coast on a cliff. Just standing in some of the offices, you can see the ocean. Imagine how beautiful it will be if the sky is clear (it’s a cloudy day today).

The interview

with Fritz

Mainly about my education, previous working experiences, and my work in Marvell.

with Marty

The interview with Marty is interesting. First he went over my resume line by line.

Then some technical questions Q: Karnaugh Maps, and how to give a minimum equation given a truth table

Q: why implement the Karnaugh minimum equation is not good for timing? A: not optimized for CMOS circuit

Q: draw layout of a 4-input NAND gate.

Q: what’s missing from the layout? A: boundary poly. (funny thing is in the interview with FlexLogix, the guy also asked about the boundary layout problem with 16nm that time the answer is PODE/CPODE)

Marty also mentioned that they are using a differential mux as basic cells.

  1. It’s layout is impact
  2. It can do almost everything: latch, adder, …
